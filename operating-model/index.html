Who's on the hook — 4 simple rules

Instead of a detailed chart for every single step, 4 plain rules that cover how AI and people share the work.

Illustrative — to be validated against the real org during discovery.
1

AI does the work, a person is on the hook

AI writes drafts and runs basic checks. But a human is always the one accountable if something goes wrong — never the AI.

2

Low-risk content: spot-checked, not reviewed one by one

The Brand Manager / Auditor doesn't approve every single piece — they check a sample and fix the rules if something's off.

3

Medium or high-risk content: a person reviews before it goes out

AI prepares the draft. A person decides if it's ready. AI never gets the final say on anything risky.

4

Legal only steps in for high-risk content — and has the final word

Low and medium-risk content never reaches Legal. When it's high-risk, Legal's decision is final.

Quality gates, by risk tier

Not every piece of content needs the same amount of checking. Riskier content gets checked more; safer content gets checked less — but there's always a clear line for what counts as good enough.

Illustrative — thresholds shown are placeholders to demonstrate the structure. Real thresholds get calibrated with the Head of Brand once sampling data exists.

Low risk

  • A computer checks it automatically and scores it
  • 1 in 20 pieces also gets a quick human check, every week
  • If too many mistakes start slipping through, this level gets stricter

Medium risk

  • A person has to sign off before it's used
  • The computer checks it first, as an early pass
  • How often things need fixing tells us if something's off

High risk

  • Nothing changes here — the full review stays exactly as it is today
  • AI only writes a first draft; a person does the rest
  • Legal always has to approve — no shortcuts, ever

Reusable assets

Shared materials so no team has to start from scratch — built once, used by everyone.

Illustrative — structure and ownership shown as an example; actual content gets populated as initiatives from Process Redesign go live.

When do these get built?

Not from scratch. The first version comes from looking at what the 3 existing teams already do informally, and turning that private know-how into something shared. Every new project adds more to it after that.

Do they change over time?

Yes, constantly. These aren't written once and forgotten — they get updated whenever something stops working.

Who's in charge of them?

Day to day, whoever owns each one below. Bigger changes — like making a rule stricter — go through the governance council first, not one person alone.

Prompt library

Owner: Brand Manager / Auditor

  • Grouped by type of content — campaign visuals, product descriptions, social posts
  • Nobody changes it alone — every update gets a second look first
  • Replaces the private notes each of the 3 teams used to keep on their own

Brand standards

Owner: Brand Manager / Auditor

  • Written so a computer can check content against it automatically
  • Same rules no matter which team or tool made the content
  • Updated when mistakes start happening too often

Playbooks

Owner: Change & Capability Lead

  • Simple step-by-step guides for things that happen again and again — like handing off work between teams
  • Written once, reused by every team that joins later
  • Updated whenever Process Redesign finds a new gap
