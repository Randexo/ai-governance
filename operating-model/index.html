Size of the prize & feasibility — the prioritization criteria

Every opportunity surfaced in the process review gets scored on these two axes before it becomes a candidate initiative.

This matrix identifies candidates — it doesn't fund them. Each one still needs a real business case: an MVP-level financial model (cost, ROI, payback), built with Finance, before it gets resourced.

Size of the prize

  • Estimated $ impact — cost saved plus revenue potential
  • Volume of content or teams it touches
  • Leverage — does it benefit one team, or many via shared infrastructure

Feasibility

  • Technical complexity and data readiness — rules-based vs. requires judgment
  • Brand-risk exposure if something goes wrong
  • Change required — new roles, retraining, process redesign needed
  • Can it show a validated result inside the 90-day window — if not, it drops into Big Bets regardless of prize size

Existing roles, redefined — not new headcount

The mistake in the 3 pilots was layering AI on top of the role without changing what the role does. Here the work is redefined, not the headcount added.

Example playbook

Quality gates, by risk tier

"Quality" isn't one bar — it's a different, explicit bar per risk tier, so automation can check against it without a human re-deciding every time.

Illustrative — thresholds shown are placeholders to demonstrate the structure. Real thresholds get calibrated with the Head of Brand once sampling data exists.

Low risk

  • Automated brand-compliance score ≥ threshold (TBD)
  • 5% human sampling pass rate tracked weekly
  • Off-brand incident rate below agreed cap

Medium risk

  • 1 brand reviewer sign-off required, logged
  • Automated compliance check as a pre-filter
  • Revision rate tracked as a quality signal

High risk

  • Full review chain unchanged — no automated gate replaces it
  • AI output treated as a first draft only
  • Legal/compliance sign-off mandatory, no exceptions

Reusable assets

Assets that get built once and reused across teams — the mechanism that turns 3 separate pilots' worth of work into a shared foundation.

A note on terminology: marketing operations distinguishes playbooks from SOPs — SOPs fit rigid, compliance-driven tasks with no room for judgment (like a legal sign-off rule); playbooks fit cross-functional, judgment-based work (like a creative handoff). Most of what this pillar produces are playbooks, not SOPs.
Illustrative — structure and ownership shown as an example; actual content gets populated as initiatives from Process Redesign go live.

Prompt library

Owner: Brand Manager / Auditor

  • Organized by solution type — campaign creative, PDP copy, social repurposing
  • Versioned — no team edits a shared prompt without review
  • Replaces each pilot's private prompt library from before the reset

Brand standards

Owner: Brand Manager / Auditor

  • Codified rules an automated QA check can run against
  • Same rules regardless of which team or tool produced the asset
  • Reviewed each time an incident rate crosses its threshold

Playbooks

Owner: Change & Capability Lead

  • Cross-functional guides for recurring situations — handoffs between teams, onboarding a new team to the shared studio
  • Written once, reused by every team that scales in
  • Updated as Process Redesign finds new gaps

Example playbook — Brand Marketing → Social handoff

A concrete example built to the standard structure marketing operations playbooks use: purpose, scope, owner, trigger, numbered steps with owner and tool, escalation path, and review cadence.

Illustrative — one example playbook, chosen because the gap it fixes is already documented in the case. The full library gets built as Process Redesign surfaces more handoffs.

Owner

Brand Manager / Auditor

Scope

Low & medium-risk campaign assets

Version

v0.1 — draft

Review cadence

Quarterly

Purpose

Eliminate re-briefing and duplicated work when a campaign asset moves from Brand Marketing to Social for repurposing.

Trigger

Brand Marketing marks an asset "ready for repurposing" in the shared asset library.

Steps

1

Tag and publish the asset

Owner: Brand Marketing team  ·  Tool: shared asset library

2

Auto-ingest into the repurposing queue

Owner: AI system, monitored by Social lead  ·  Tool: repurposing engine

3

Generate platform variants against the brand-as-code ruleset

Owner: AI system  ·  Tool: prompt library, brand standards

4

Route by risk tier

Owner: automated routing  ·  low risk → 5% sampling; medium risk → 1 reviewer

5

Publish

Owner: automated for low risk; Social lead confirms for medium risk

Escalation

If a variant fails its brand-compliance score: routes automatically to the Brand Manager / Auditor for manual review within 24 hours — it does not sit in a queue silently.
