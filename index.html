1Transformation Lead — held by the COO
  • Chairs the weekly action meeting and owns the single source of truth
  • Removes roadblocks
  • Early on: chases key data points (e.g. agency spend, SKU volume, brand risk, licensing cost) to build the opportunity inventory
  • Later: coaches initiative owners and tracks progress against agreed criteria
2CMO — sponsor
  • Owns the board relationship and the business case for the transformation
  • Makes sure the numbers are credible before they go to the board
  • Decides which opportunities to present to the board as they mature
3CTO — sponsor (technology)
  • Elevated to full sponsor given the weight of the technology consolidation
  • Early on: supplies negotiated licensing cost data
  • Later: approves the technology standard per solution type
4Head of Brand — voting member
  • Holds veto over the brand-risk criterion in the approval redesign
  • Early on: supplies brand-risk classification by category
  • Later: signs off on risk thresholds before sampling rates change
5Finance — value assurance
  • Independent check on the ROI math
  • Early on: supplies agency-spend data and validates the directional ROI range
  • Later: certifies net ROI per solution before it enters the scorecard
6Change & capability lead — held by C&T initially
  • Held by C&T during ramp-up while coaching an internal successor in parallel
  • Early on: runs comms so original teams stay informed
  • Later: co-leads training as momentum builds, then hands off fully
7Team leads — one per brand team / function
  • Active from the start with a role that evolves
  • Early on: identifies and prioritizes each team's opportunities
  • Later: takes direct ownership of what gets built from that work

Team-level standups run from week one — early on they track diagnostic progress and help shape candidate initiatives; once initiatives are prioritized, the same rhythm shifts to tracking delivery.

Steering committee

Higher-altitude checkpoint. Translates Office progress into the board-facing narrative.

TO weekly action meeting

Fast, fact-based. Removes roadblocks, tracks the single source of truth. 60–90 min.

Team standups

Same slot, different job over time: early on it tracks diagnostic progress and helps surface candidate opportunities; later it becomes a day-to-day delivery check for the team's own work.

Meeting name
AI Transformation — Steering Committee
Primary objective
Translate TO progress into the board-facing value narrative and resolve any decisions that require sponsor authority

Specific objectives

  • Review portfolio health at altitude
  • Validate the value story is credible and board-ready
  • Approve escalations from the TO that require sponsor authority
  • Calibrate priorities and direction for the coming month

Meeting details

Day: Last Monday of the month

Duration: 60 min

Frequency: Monthly

Format: In-person preferred

Meeting rules

  • Only Finance-certified figures are presented upward
  • Transformation Lead circulates a one-pager 48 h before
  • Decisions made here take precedence over TO-level decisions
  • Minutes distributed to all TO members within 24 hours

KPIs reviewed

  • Portfolio completion vs. plan (% initiatives on track)
  • Cumulative certified ROI vs. projected
  • # escalations resolved vs. still pending
  • Board narrative readiness (ready / draft / not started)

Meeting details

Day: Monday

Duration: 60–90 min

Frequency: Weekly

Format: In-person / virtual

Meeting rules

  • Only reviewed data updated before the session starts
  • Every owner arrives with status and blockers prepared
  • Hard stop at 90 minutes
  • Every agreement is logged with owner and deadline

KPIs reviewed

  • % blockers resolved this week
  • Progress vs. approval criteria per initiative
  • Status of the 4 key data requests (Sprint 1)
Meeting name
Team Initiative Standup
Primary objective
Keep delivery on track within each team — surface blockers before they reach the weekly TO meeting

Specific objectives

  • Check progress against the week's commitments
  • Surface blockers before they compound
  • Align on the next 48-hour priorities
  • Keep the team's section of the source of truth current

Meeting details

Days: Tuesday & Thursday

Duration: 15–20 min

Frequency: 2× per week

Format: Standing / virtual

Meeting rules

  • Three questions only: done / doing / blocked
  • No decks, no status reports — spoken updates only
  • Blockers unresolved after 24 h go to the TO weekly
  • Team Lead updates the source of truth immediately after

KPIs reviewed

  • # tasks completed vs. committed (last period)
  • # blockers open > 24 hours
  • Velocity vs. initiative milestone
  • Team health check (1–5, optional)
