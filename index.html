Transformation Office — reference artifact

Roles and meeting cadence

A standing governing body with real decision rights over AI priorities, standards, and resourcing. "Transformation Lead" avoids collision with the Chief Technology Officer stakeholder — the role itself is held by the COO, not a new hire.

Who sits at the table, and why

Click a seat or a card to match them. All 7 seats are active from Sprint 1 — several roles simply do a different job once the portfolio is selected in Sprint 2.

1Transformation Lead — held by the COO

Runs the Office day to day: chairs the weekly action meeting, owns the single source of truth, removes roadblocks. Natural extension of the COO's existing mandate over the operating-model redesign — not a new job on top of an old one.

Sprint 1

Runs discovery: chases the 4 data requests, builds the inventory

Sprint 2+

Runs the portfolio: coaches initiative owners, tracks the gate criteria

2CMO — sponsor

Owns the board relationship and the value story. Deliberately kept out of day-to-day operating: needs focus for the external narrative, not distraction from weekly execution meetings.

Sprint 1

Confirms the directional ROI is credible before it's shared upward

Sprint 2+

Signs off on which initiatives get the value-story treatment

3CTO — sponsor (technology)

Chief Technology Officer — not to be confused with the Transformation Lead. Elevated to full sponsor given the weight of the consolidation decision.

Sprint 1

Supplies negotiated licensing cost data

Sprint 2+

Approves the technology standard per solution type

4Head of Brand — voting member

Not a general sponsor — holds veto specifically over the brand-risk criterion in the approval redesign, a functional seat, not a broad one.

Sprint 1

Supplies the brand-risk classification by category

Sprint 2+

Signs off on risk thresholds before sampling rates change

5Finance — value assurance

Independent check on the ROI math — validates that every reported number is real, not a vanity figure.

Sprint 1

Supplies agency-spend data; sanity-checks the directional ROI range

Sprint 2+

Certifies net ROI per solution before it enters the scorecard

6Change & capability lead — held by C&T initially

Filled by C&T (the engagement team) during ramp-up, coaching an internal successor in parallel. Hands off fully once that person is ready — not a permanent external seat.

Sprint 1

C&T runs comms so original teams don't feel sidelined during discovery

Sprint 2+

Internal successor co-leads training as quick wins launch; C&T steps back

7Team leads — one per brand team / function

A continuous role that evolves, not a seat that appears from nothing. Present from Sprint 1, with a different job at each stage.

Sprint 1

Identify and prioritize their own team's opportunities for the matrix

Sprint 2+

Become the initiative owner directing their own selected initiative

Steering committee

Higher-altitude checkpoint. Translates Office progress into the board-facing narrative.

TO weekly action meeting

Fast, fact-based. Removes roadblocks, tracks the single source of truth. 60–90 min.

Initiative standups

Day-to-day delivery check within each initiative team — not an Office meeting. 15–20 min.
