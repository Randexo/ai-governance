Roles and meeting cadence

A standing governing body with real decision rights over AI priorities, standards, and resourcing. "Transformation Lead" avoids collision with the Chief Technology Officer stakeholder — the role itself is held by the COO, not a new hire.

Who sits at the table, and why

Click a seat or a card to match them. All 7 seats are active from the start — several roles simply do a different job once initiatives are selected and prioritized.

1Transformation Lead — held by the COO

Runs the Office day to day: chairs the weekly action meeting, owns the single source of truth, and removes roadblocks — a natural extension of the COO's existing mandate over the operating-model redesign, not a new job on top of an old one. Early on, that means chasing 4 key data points (agency spend, SKU volume, brand risk, licensing cost) to build the inventory; later, it means coaching whoever owns each opportunity and tracking progress against agreed criteria.

2CMO — sponsor

Owns the board relationship and the value story. Deliberately kept out of day-to-day operating — needs focus for the external narrative, not distraction from weekly execution meetings. Confirms the directional ROI is credible before it's shared upward, and later signs off on which opportunities get the value-story treatment as they mature.

3CTO — sponsor (technology)

Chief Technology Officer — not to be confused with the Transformation Lead. Elevated to full sponsor given the weight of the consolidation decision. Supplies negotiated licensing cost data early on, then approves the technology standard per solution type as the work matures.

4Head of Brand — voting member

Not a general sponsor — holds veto specifically over the brand-risk criterion in the approval redesign, a functional seat, not a broad one. Supplies the brand-risk classification by category early on, then signs off on risk thresholds before sampling rates change.

5Finance — value assurance

Independent check on the ROI math — validates that every reported number is real, not a vanity figure. Supplies agency-spend data and sanity-checks the directional ROI range early on, then certifies net ROI per solution before it enters the scorecard.

6Change & capability lead — held by C&T initially

Filled by C&T (the engagement team) during ramp-up, coaching an internal successor in parallel — hands off fully once that person is ready, not a permanent external seat. Runs comms early on so original teams don't feel sidelined, then co-leads training with the internal successor as momentum builds.

7Team leads — one per brand team / function

A continuous role that evolves, not a seat that appears from nothing. Active from the start, with a different job over time: early on, they identify and prioritize their own team's opportunities; later, they take direct ownership of what gets built from that work.
